Game Design

Hate

This game is an abstract expression of the word hate. I wanted to distill the emotion into a form that would be identifiable outside of human interaction so chose to go the route of abstraction.

Play online!

An explanation for those who want it:

In the game, the player controls a red star with the arrow keys. When the player's star touches a yellow circle, the circle yelps and dims. A mob of white stars will automatically attack the most recently touched circle, further dimming it. As the player continues to hit the circles, the players movement becomes more erratic and moves with jerky movements within a radius. Eventually, all circles will dim to gray and the game is concluded.

The chaotic sound of an angry mob along with a high-pitched helpless yelping aims to disrupt the player's thoughts and push them towards a singular goal.

Petals Adrift

Petals Adrift is a 5-level beat-matching game for Windows that I worked on over a couple weeks, based on a prototype I made with a friend along with art by some other friends. You can find the initial sketches, refined maps, and implemented levels for the first three below. If you'd like to play, check it out on itch.io above! Donkey Kong Country

This is a study I did of Donkey Kong Country's opening level: Jungle Hijinx. This was, and still is, one of my favorite games to play! I took notes as I played through the level, analyzing the various aspects of the level design and also paying attention to the user journey. Annotations include my inner monologue and affective state at each point.
